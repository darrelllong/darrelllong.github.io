The only thing that you truly have is time.

People say that time is money. That is exactly the opposite of the truth. Money is what people give you in exchange for your time. You trade little pieces of your life in order to get money.

We have always had to spend part of our time doing things we would rather not do: hunting, gathering, digging in a mine, and the other things we need to do in order simply to live. But in the modern world, we have jobs, and that is where we trade parts of our lives to get the money we need in order to live, and to use to do the things that we enjoy.

So time is not money. Money is a proxy for the time you traded to some other person — a fungible asset that you can exchange to live, and to buy things that you enjoy.
